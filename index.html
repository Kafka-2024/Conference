Kafka 2024


One Hundred Years of Kafka

Walter Benjamin argued that to understand Kafka's work,
one must consider Kafka’s sense of failure, evident in his wish to destroy his writings.
This failure suggests that interpretations of his work might also be flawed.
Kafka used this sense of failure to envision a new humanity, finding emerging laws
and relationships without guarantees—ironic given his insurance job.
Like his character Hunter Gracchus, Kafka was an outsider, seeing 'messianic splinters'
—small signs of future transformation amid present chaos—from his unique perspective.
